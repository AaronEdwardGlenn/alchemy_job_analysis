PDX Area Job Market Analysis for Alchemy Alumni

Data scraped from glassdoor.com, all entries listed in the PDX area from July 20th - August 3rd 2020.

265 data points were collected

*Note that a value of -1 denotes unavailable information.

Job titles:

Here we can see that most job titles contain 'software engineer'. This information could be useful when deciding what title to give yourself in your resume.


Location:

Most jobs listed are in PDX proper.


Headquarters:

While headquarters are primarily located in PDX, this also widely varies.


Year Founded:


Industry:

Here we can see the most common industries hiring right now.


Firm Size:

Firm size is not directly correlated to job offer quantity.


Type of Ownership:

Private firms are hiring at nearly double the rate of public firms.


Who is hiring?:

This breaks down the top 25 firms currently hiring.


Business Sector:

Info tech and business services dominate the current hiring sectors.


Revenue:

We can see here that, on average, the companies with the highest revenue are creating the most hiring positions.


Common Alchemy Stacks in Job Descriptions:

These charts show the likelihood of a hiring firm's job description containing Javascript, MongoDB/mongoose, Node, React, or Webpack.


Average Salaries:

This table shows us the average salaries overall, and includes the average rating, year founded, age of firm, description length, and number of competitors listed.


Average Salary Based on Job Title and Seniority:

This chart shows us the break down of what the average salaries are for jobs based on how the title of the job is defined and the seniority of the job.


Correlation Heatmap:

This heatmap shows us the corollary relationship between categories. If you look at the top left of the Y-axis, you can follow 'average_salary' and see which squares are darker on the X-axis. This conveys a stronger correlation.



I hope you find this information useful or at least interesting. The number of datapoints is pretty low so the conclusions made here are far from definitive of broader trends. However, it does give us some insight on the current state of the job market for developers in the PDX area.
